# Crypto Fiduciary Powers

Crypto Fiduciary Powers

Trust Exhibit A:
(24) Cryptocurrency & Digital Asset Powers (supplementing ¶¶7 and 10; this provision controls in any conflict).
(a) 	Definitions. For this paragraph:
(i) “Crypto Asset” means any blockchain-based asset or right (including cryptocurrency, stablecoins, tokens, wrapped assets, NFTs, DAO tokens, liquid-staking tokens, restaking derivatives, and any on-chain claim), and any proceeds or forks thereof.
(ii) “Wallet” means any address, account, or arrangement (including hardware, software, MPC, multisig, custodial, or smart-contract wallet) that holds or controls Crypto Assets.
(iii) “Key Material” means any private key, seed phrase, shard, passcode, recovery method, or credential that confers control or transfer authority.
(iv) “Custodian” means any exchange or third-party wallet/custody provider.
(v) “Protocol Action” means any on-chain or off-chain interaction with a blockchain network or smart contract (including transfers, swaps, approvals/allowances, staking/unstaking, delegations, governance voting, claims, bridging, minting, burning, wrapping/unwrapping, providing/removing liquidity, borrowing/lending, and redeeming).
(vi) “Network Event” means an airdrop, fork, chain split, merge, reorg, token migration, redenomination, or similar event.
(b) Custody; Key Management. The Trustee may self-custody or use one or more Custodians; create, maintain, rotate, shard, back up, and retire Wallets and Key Material; set and change multisig/MPC thresholds and signers; conduct key ceremonies; and adopt and modify a written key-management and security policy (including cold storage, hardware-wallet use, geographic dispersion of backups, and signer whitelists/blacklists). The Trustee may withhold Key Material from beneficiaries and accountings if disclosure would create a material risk of loss; view-only addresses or redacted exhibits may be used to satisfy reporting.
(c) Exchanges, Accounts, and Terms. The Trustee may open, maintain, and close accounts with any Custodian; complete KYC/AML, provide required certifications, and agree to and rely upon terms of service; consolidate or migrate holdings between Wallets and Custodians; and select venues and jurisdictions the Trustee deems prudent.
(d) Protocol Actions; DeFi; Bridges. The Trustee may execute any Protocol Action, including interacting with centralized or decentralized exchanges; performing swaps and transfers; approving token allowances; providing or removing liquidity; using money markets; borrowing, lending, posting collateral, and managing margin; minting, burning, wrapping and unwrapping assets; and using bridges and cross-chain messaging. The Trustee may pay any gas, validator, bridge, or protocol fee from trust assets.
(e) Staking, Validation, Mining, and Restaking. The Trustee may stake, delegate, compound, and un-stake assets; select validators or run validator infrastructure (directly or via service providers); claim, compound, and restake rewards; participate in slashing-protected or insured programs; and engage in mining or similar activities, including purchasing, leasing, or hosting equipment and paying related costs (hardware, energy, colocation, insurance, maintenance).
(f) NFTs and Digital Media. The Trustee may mint, buy, sell, list, delist, or fractionalize NFTs; pay for metadata storage or pinning (e.g., IPFS/Arweave); manage or license associated intellectual-property rights and royalties; and take steps to preserve media availability.
(g) DAOs and Governance. The Trustee may exercise governance or voting rights (including snapshot/off-chain votes), delegate voting, submit proposals, accept or decline token or NFT airdrops, and take any steps necessary to evidence or protect the trust’s interests in DAOs or similar organizations.
(h) Forks, Airdrops, and Network Events. The Trustee may recognize or disregard a Network Event; claim, abandon, or sell resulting assets; select which chain to support after a split; and perform any required steps (signatures, proofs, snapshots) to perfect title.
(i) Valuation; Accounting; Allocation. The Trustee may determine valuation methodologies for Crypto Assets (including using or averaging independent pricing services, exchange quotes, or oracles) and may treat hard-to-value assets at cost or nominal value pending market discovery. The Trustee may allocate receipts and expenses (including gas and fees) between income and principal in the Trustee’s discretion consistent with applicable principal-and-income law. Without limiting the foregoing: staking/validator rewards, interest-like yields, and lending income may be treated as income; airdrops, forks, and similar Network Events may be treated as principal (unless local law or accounting conventions indicate otherwise). The Trustee’s good-faith allocations and valuations shall be final and binding.
(j) Tax and Reporting. The Trustee may select and change any reasonable lot-relief and basis method (including specific identification, FIFO, LIFO, or average), engage tax software and blockchain analytics providers, make and revoke tax elections, file any required information returns, and retain experts. The Trustee may determine character and timing of income and gains, including the treatment of staking/validation rewards, airdrops, and forks; and may withhold or pay any taxes due.
(k) Risk Management; Concentration; Insurance. The Trustee may retain concentrated positions in Crypto Assets, is not required to diversify merely due to volatility, and may obtain or decline insurance (including crime, custody, or slashing coverage). The Trustee shall not be liable for losses arising from market volatility, exchange or custodian failure, protocol bugs, smart-contract exploits, chain reorganizations, slashing, bridges, or similar crypto-native risks, provided the Trustee acts in good faith and with the care a prudent person would exercise under similar circumstances.
(l) Compliance. The Trustee may implement commercially reasonable compliance measures (including OFAC/sanctions screening, geofencing, wallet-screening services, and travel-rule compliance via vendors) and may decline any transaction that, in the Trustee’s discretion, poses legal, regulatory, or sanction risk.
(m) Successor Transition; Emergency Actions. The Trustee may maintain a sealed inventory of Wallets and procedures for emergency access; upon resignation, removal, incapacity, or death, may deliver Key Material and control via secure means to a successor, including temporary freeze or timelock measures. The Trustee may appoint agents to assist with emergency recovery.
(n) Delegation. The Trustee may delegate any authority in this paragraph to qualified agents, sub-custodians, validators, administrators, or advisors and rely in good faith on their reports, audits, and attestations.
(o) No Admission of Classification. References to “securities,” “commodities,” or similar classifications are for convenience only and shall not be deemed an admission of regulatory status.
