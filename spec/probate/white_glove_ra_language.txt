1. Parties and Scope of Representation
This Representation Agreement (“Agreement”) is entered into on |current-date-month-day-year|, by and between Speedwell Law, PLLC (“Law Firm”) and |fullname| (“Client”) (collectively, the “Parties”).
The Law Firm agrees to represent the Client in the Client’s capacity as duly appointed Personal Representative of the Estate of |Contact-Decedent| (the “Estate”).
The scope of representation includes advising the Client regarding the administration of the Estate and assisting the Client in carrying out their duties as Personal Representative under Virginia law. The Law Firm is not being engaged to serve as a fiduciary and shall not act as Personal Representative.
Services outside the scope of this Agreement may be performed only upon mutual agreement of the Parties.

2. Fees and Costs
A. Retainer Fee
The Client agrees to pay an initial Retainer Fee of $3,000, which is a minimum fee required to commence representation. This fee is earned upon receipt and is intended to cover initial administrative work, including assistance with qualification of the Client as Personal Representative and related onboarding tasks.
The Retainer Fee will be applied as a credit toward amounts owed to the Law Firm as described below.

B. Administrative Service Fee
In addition to legal services, the Law Firm offers enhanced administrative support services designed to assist the Client in performing the non-discretionary, administrative aspects of estate administration (the “Service Fee”).
The Service Fee is described in Exhibit A and is calculated with reference to customary fiduciary compensation guidelines utilized by the Fairfax Commissioner of Accounts solely as a benchmark for estimating administrative effort and complexity. The Service Fee:
	•	is not a fiduciary fee; 
	•	is not paid from or contingent upon fiduciary compensation actually taken by the Client; 
	•	is owed independently of whether the Client elects to claim or waive fiduciary compensation; and 
	•	compensates the Law Firm for administrative and support services that do not constitute legal representation. 
The Service Fee shall be billed in stages corresponding to major estate administration milestones, including qualification, preparation and filing of the Inventory, and preparation of accountings, unless otherwise specified in Exhibit A.
Any remaining balance of the Service Fee shall be due within thirty (30) days of invoicing.

C. Legal Fees (Hourly Work)
Legal services provided by the Law Firm are billed separately on an hourly basis at the rates set forth in Exhibit A.
Legal services include, but are not limited to:
	•	legal research and analysis; 
	•	preparation of pleadings and legal documents; 
	•	court filings; 
	•	representation in contested matters; 
	•	appearances before a court or the Commissioner of Accounts; and 
	•	advising the Client regarding legal rights and obligations. 
Non-adversarial administrative assistance—such as document preparation, coordination of filings, and routine estate administration support—is generally covered by the Service Fee. However, if a matter becomes adversarial, involves court intervention, or requires legal judgment beyond routine administration, such work will be billed as legal services.
Time is billed in six (6) minute increments. Travel time may be billed at one-half the applicable hourly rate, plus mileage reimbursement.

D. Costs and Expenses
The Client is responsible for all third-party costs and expenses (“Costs”) incurred in connection with the Estate, including but not limited to:
	•	court filing fees; 
	•	publication costs; 
	•	service of process fees; 
	•	recording fees; 
	•	postage and delivery expenses; 
	•	third-party service providers (e.g., appraisers, accountants, or deed preparation services). 
Such Costs may be advanced by the Law Firm and reimbursed by the Client, or billed directly to the Client.

E. Nature of Services and Allocation of Responsibilities
The Law Firm provides a high-support administrative service model intended to reduce the Client’s administrative burden to the extent permitted by law. However:
	•	The Client remains the sole fiduciary and retains all authority, discretion, and responsibility associated with the role of Personal Representative. 
	•	The Law Firm does not assume fiduciary duties and does not exercise independent control over estate decisions. 
	•	The Client is responsible for all fiduciary decisions, including but not limited to marshaling assets, payment of debts, and distributions to beneficiaries. 
The Law Firm will assist the Client in carrying out these responsibilities but will act only at the Client’s direction with respect to fiduciary matters.
The Client agrees to cooperate with the Law Firm and to provide timely information and decisions necessary to facilitate estate administration.

F. Professional Responsibilities and Limitations
Nothing in this Agreement limits the Law Firm’s obligations under the Virginia Rules of Professional Conduct. The Law Firm will not advise or assist the Client in any conduct the Law Firm reasonably believes to be unlawful or inconsistent with the Client’s fiduciary duties.

G. Scheduling and Administrative Policies
Meetings cancelled by the Client with less than twenty-four (24) hours’ notice may be subject to a $100 cancellation fee.

H. Additional Services
Work outside the scope of this Agreement may be performed upon mutual agreement of the Parties, which may be confirmed in writing or by email. 
Exhibit B – Client Responsibilities and Expectations
This Exhibit B (“Client Expectations Agreement”) is incorporated into and made part of the Representation Agreement between Speedwell Law, PLLC (“Law Firm”) and |full-name| (“Client”).

1. Communication and Scheduling
The Client agrees to communicate with the Law Firm in a timely and professional manner.
	•	If the Client requires prompt assistance, the Client shall contact the Law Firm’s Probate Paralegal at probate@speedwelllaw.com, who will coordinate an appropriate response. 
	•	Scheduled meetings cancelled with less than one (1) business day’s notice may be subject to a $100 cancellation fee. 

2. Role of the Client as Fiduciary
The Client acknowledges and agrees that:
	•	The Client serves as Administrator, Executor, or Trustee (as applicable) and remains the sole fiduciary of the Estate or Trust. 
	•	Certain fiduciary duties cannot be delegated, including the exercise of judgment and decision-making authority. 
	•	The Client must devote sufficient time, attention, and effort to: 
	•	marshaling assets, 
	•	administering the estate or trust, and 
	•	making distributions in accordance with applicable law. 
The Client remains legally responsible for all fiduciary actions and decisions.

3. Protection and Management of Estate Assets
The Client agrees to act promptly and prudently to:
	•	identify and secure estate assets, 
	•	safeguard such assets from loss, waste, or mismanagement, and 
	•	ensure proper handling of estate property. 
The Law Firm may assist in coordinating these efforts, but does not assume control or custody of estate assets.

4. Coordination with the Law Firm
The Client agrees to:
	•	supervise and review the Law Firm’s work; 
	•	provide accurate and complete information upon request; 
	•	respond promptly to communications and document requests; and 
	•	collaborate with the Law Firm as reasonably necessary to complete estate administration. 
The Client understands that delays in cooperation may result in delays in administration and additional costs.

5. Preparation of Fiduciary Reports
The Law Firm will assist in preparing certain fiduciary filings for a non-adversarial estate administration, which may include:
	•	qualification documents, 
	•	Affidavit of Notice, 
	•	Inventory of the Estate, and 
	•	Accountings submitted to the Commissioner of Accounts. 
However, the Client acknowledges and agrees that:
	•	the Client remains ultimately responsible for the accuracy and completeness of all filings; 
	•	the Client is legally responsible for ensuring that all required reports are timely submitted to the appropriate parties, including the court, Commissioner of Accounts, and beneficiaries. 

6. Tax Matters
The Client acknowledges that:
	•	the Client is responsible for ensuring that all required tax returns are prepared and filed, including: 
	•	the decedent’s final individual income tax return, and 
	•	any required estate or fiduciary income tax returns. 
The Law Firm does not prepare tax returns.
The Law Firm may assist the Client in identifying and coordinating with qualified tax professionals, but assumes no responsibility for tax preparation or tax advice unless separately agreed in writing.

7. Estate Expenses and Debts
The Law Firm may assist in coordinating and facilitating the resolution of estate debts and expenses. However:
	•	the Client remains solely responsible for determining whether and when to pay claims; 
	•	the Client is responsible for ensuring proper payment of valid debts and expenses; and 
	•	the Client retains responsibility for protecting estate assets in connection with such payments. 

8. Estate Financial Accounts
The Client agrees to:
	•	establish an estate bank account as required; 
	•	ensure that all estate-related financial activity is conducted through that account; and 
	•	arrange for account statements to be made available to the Law Firm (including, where feasible, by providing duplicate statements or electronic access). 

9. Communications with Third Parties
The Client agrees that:
	•	communications with opposing or potentially adverse parties should be conducted through the Law Firm where appropriate; 
	•	the Client will not communicate directly with any represented party regarding estate matters without that party’s counsel present or without prior coordination with the Law Firm. 

10. Acknowledgment
By signing below, the Client acknowledges that they have read, understood, and agree to the responsibilities and expectations set forth in this Exhibit.
