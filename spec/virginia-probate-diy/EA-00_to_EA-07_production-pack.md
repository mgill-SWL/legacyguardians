# EA‑01 — The Virginia Probate Glossary (executor/admin focus)

**Objective:** Give the minimum vocabulary needed to understand the process.

### Slide plan
1) Title: *Glossary: the 12 terms you need*
- We’ll keep this practical

2) Executor vs Administrator
- Executor = named in will
- Administrator = appointed when no will (or executor unavailable)

3) Testate vs Intestate
- Testate: will
- Intestate: no will

4) “Qualify” / “Qualification”
- The court gives you legal authority
- Authority ≠ ownership

5) Letters / Certificate
- Proof you can act
- Banks/companies will ask for it

6) Heirs vs Beneficiaries
- Heirs: intestacy law
- Beneficiaries: will

7) Probate estate vs non‑probate assets
- Some assets bypass probate
- Don’t open probate “just because”

8) Fiduciary duty
- You’re handling someone else’s money
- Document everything

9) Commissioner of Accounts (COA)
- Oversight + reporting
- Inventory/accountings

10) Bond/surety (high level)
- Sometimes required
- It’s about protection, not punishment

11) Practical takeaway
- Your job is administration + documentation

12) Next episode preview
- EA‑02: probate vs non-probate

### Fairfax note
- Fairfax COA tends to be strict about clean documentation and timely filings; your recordkeeping system matters.

### Slide plan
1) Title: *Real property in Virginia: “drops like a rock”*
- Why this matters early

2) The core idea (plain English)
- Title can vest immediately at death
- That doesn’t end the admin process

3) Title vs authority vs responsibility
- Who owns vs who can sign vs who must maintain

4) Testate vs intestate (high-level)
- Who it vests in depends on will vs heirs

5) What the executor/admin can do (and can’t)
- Access/maintenance vs sale/transfer
- Don’t promise a buyer anything yet

6) When selling real property becomes necessary
- Paying debts
- Splitting among multiple heirs
- Practical reasons

7) Mortgage/insurance/HOA
- Keep it from defaulting
- Keep coverage valid

8) Inventory/accounting implications (high-level)
- “Not everything you own is an estate asset for administration”
- Documentation still matters

9) Example 1: simple will
- House to child; other assets in bank
- What still needs probate and why

10) Example 2: intestate with multiple heirs
- Why sale can become necessary

11) 🛑 Stop points
- Unclear title
- Disputed heirs
- Need to sell quickly

12) Next episode
