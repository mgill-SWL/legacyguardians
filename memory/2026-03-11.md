## Bloodline trust distribution scheme — coaching notes (Legacy Guardians)
- Bloodline trust distribution scheme **replaces the residual distribution clause**.
- Purpose: hold the rest, remainder, and residue in continuing trust across multiple generations to benefit the settlor’s descendants.
- Common staging patterns for beneficiary access / principal withdrawal rights (ROW):
  - Typical: **Net income at 21** + **ROW at 25**.
  - Sometimes unify: **net income + ROW both at 25**.
  - Sometimes split ROW:
    - 2 tranches: **25 / 30** (equal chunks).
    - 3 tranches (generally the max for non-UHNW): **25 / 30 / 35** (thirds).
  - UHNW estates: may delay ROW as far as **40**.
- User can provide template language for delayed principal withdrawal arrangements if not already included in provided docs.
- Residual distribution preference when there are multiple children:
  - Strong preference for **"per capita at each generation"** when there are **3+ children**; always raised when there are **4 children**.
  - Rationale: fairness at the generational level; avoids unintended disparities driven by how many children each child has.
